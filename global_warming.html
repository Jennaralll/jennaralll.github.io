First Mammal Species Goes Extinct Due to Climate Change

A small rodent that lived only on a single island off Australia is likely the world's first mammal to be a casualty of climate change. Also called the "mosaic-tailed rat", the rodent is named after its home on Bramble Cay, an island that is ten feet above sea level.
